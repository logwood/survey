First, this survey introduces a layered taxonomy of embodied datasets, and then analyzes the current data production ecosystem in depth, showing how acquisition settings, simulation environments, and large-scale data pipelines collectively generate and sustain the datasets and benchmarks used in modern robotic manipulation.
Second, it proposes a dual-pathway learning decomposition that separates cognitive planning and policy learning, and then analyzes how different learning paradigms fit into this decomposition and what trade-offs they entail.
Third, it treats embodied manipulation as a system problem, analyzing how embodiment constraints, data regimes, learning architectures, and evaluation protocols are mutually coupled and showing how choices at one stage propagate through the rest of the pipeline.


First, this survey first introduces a layered taxonomy of embodied datasets, and then analyzes the current data production ecosystem in depth, showing how acquisition settings, simulation environments, and large-scale data pipelines collectively generate and sustain the datasets and benchmarks used in modern robotic manipulation.
Second, it separates cognitive planning and policy learning, and analyzes how different learning paradigms fit into this decomposition, how they are integrated into complete manipulation systems, and what trade-offs they entail for deployment.
Third, it treats embodied manipulation as a system problem, analyzing how embodiment constraints, data regimes, learning architectures, deployment requirements, and evaluation protocols are mutually coupled, and how these components jointly shape system behavior and real-world performance.
