However, iterative denoising incurs prohibitive inference latency, motivating a shift toward
\textbf{Flow Matching}, which learns velocity fields transporting noise to data along straight
trajectories and admits far fewer ODE integration steps~\cite{hu2024adaflow}.
\textbf{AdaFlow}~\cite{hu2024adaflow} pioneered this direction by representing policies as
state-conditioned probability flows and introducing a variance-adaptive ODE solver that
automatically reduces to a one-step generator for unimodal action distributions.
Extending this to non-Euclidean spaces, \textbf{RFMP} embeds flow matching on Riemannian
manifolds to respect the geometric structure of robot end-effector poses, while its successor
\textbf{SRFMP}~\cite{braun2024riemannian, ding2025fast} augments these dynamics with
LaSalle-based stability guarantees, yielding smoother trajectories and robustness to
integration-horizon sensitivity.

Two orthogonal strategies have since emerged to push toward single-step inference.
The first distills pre-trained generative policies: \textbf{Consistency Policy}~\cite{prasad2024consistency}
enforces self-consistency along a teacher Diffusion Policy's ODE trajectory to produce
few-step visuomotor policies suitable for resource-constrained hardware.
The second rectifies the flow itself: \textbf{RecFlow Policy}~\cite{recflow_policy_2025}
learns a deterministic noise-action coupling via iterative flow rectification, while
\textbf{SeFA-Policy}~\cite{sefa_policy_2024} addresses the observation-inconsistency
that accumulates across reflow iterations through selective alignment against expert
demonstrations, recovering accuracy while reducing inference latency by over 98\%.

Concurrent work explores complementary axes of the speed--quality trade-off.
\textbf{FlowPolicy}~\cite{zhang2025flowpolicy} conditions on 3D point clouds and normalizes
velocity-field self-consistency to achieve single-step inference with a 7$\times$ speedup
over diffusion baselines.
\textbf{Streaming Flow Policy}~\cite{jiang2025streaming} recasts the flow process in action
space directly, sampling from a narrow Gaussian around the last executed action and streaming
actions to the robot on-the-fly during ODE integration, tightening the sensorimotor loop
for receding-horizon control.
\textbf{ReSeFlow}~\cite{wang2025reseflow} injects rectified-flow efficiency into SE(3)-equivariant
diffusion models, achieving geodesic-consistent one-step generation while preserving
rotational and translational symmetry for unstructured environments.
\textbf{ManiFlow}~\cite{yan2025maniflow} scales consistency flow training to multi-modal
inputs via the proposed DiT-X architecture with adaptive cross-attention, achieving 1--2
step dexterous action generation across single-arm, bimanual, and humanoid platforms.
Finally, \textbf{Guided Flow Policy}~\cite{tiofack2026guided} extends flow matching to
offline RL by coupling a value-aware behavior cloning flow with a distilled one-step actor,
selectively regularizing toward high-return transitions rather than imitating the dataset
indiscriminately.
