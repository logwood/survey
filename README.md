# Robotic Arm Manipulation toward Embodied Intelligence: Foundations, Data, Learning, and Evaluation

> A survey ready to submit to ACM Computing Surveys (CSUR)

## Abstract

Robotic arm manipulation has advanced rapidly with the emergence of deep learning, foundation models, and large language models, giving rise to a diverse landscape of learning paradigms and system designs. This survey takes embodiment constraints as the starting point, tracing how they shape the development of embodied intelligence across data and knowledge support, learning paradigms, and evaluation. By tracing these constraints across the full stack — from hardware embodiment to fine-grained evaluation metrics — this survey aims to provide a structured understanding of robotic arm manipulation and to clarify the central challenges for building scalable, deployable, and trustworthy systems.

## Survey Structure

```
Embodiment  →  Data & Knowledge  →  Learning Paradigms  →  Evaluation
```

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| Embodiment | Hardware constraints, end-effectors, sensory configurations |
| Data & Knowledge | Datasets, simulation, teleoperation, foundation model knowledge |
| Learning Paradigms | Imitation learning, reinforcement learning, VLA models |
| Evaluation | Benchmarks, metrics, reproducibility |

## Scope

This survey focuses on **robotic arm manipulation** within the embodied intelligence paradigm. For surveys on dexterous hand manipulation, we refer readers to complementary work in that direction.
